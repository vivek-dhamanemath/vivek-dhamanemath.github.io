About My Chess Passion

How the royal game has influenced my thinking

My journey with chess began in early school days, when I first learned how the pieces move. What started as a casual interest quickly evolved into a passion for strategic thinking and problem-solving.

Chess has taught me patience, forward thinking, and how to analyze complex situations. The beauty of chess lies in its perfect balance of creativity and logic – each game is different, yet follows the same fundamental principles.

"Chess is the gymnasium of the mind."

- Blaise Pascal

Today, I enjoy playing both casual and competitive games, analyzing famous matches, and continuously learning new openings and strategies.

My Favorite Chess Players

These legendary chess players have inspired me with their unique styles, strategic brilliance, and contributions to the world of chess.

Viswanathan Anand

Viswanathan "Vishy" Anand is an Indian chess grandmaster. Anand is a five-time World Chess Champion, a two-time World Rapid Chess Champion and a two-time Chess World Cup Champion. He became the first grandmaster from India in 1988, and he has the eighth-highest peak FIDE rating of all time. In 2022, he was the elected Deputy President of FIDE.

5× World Champion GM since 1988

Gukesh D

Gukesh Dommaraju also known as Gukesh D, is an Indian chess grandmaster and the reigning World Chess Champion. A chess prodigy, Gukesh is the youngest undisputed world champion, the youngest player to have surpassed a FIDE rating of 2750, doing so at the age of 17, and the third-youngest to have surpassed 2700 Elo at the age of 16.

World Champion Chess Prodigy

Magnus Carlsen

Magnus Carlsen is a Norwegian chess grandmaster. Carlsen is a five-time World Chess Champion, five-time World Rapid Chess Champion, the reigning (shared with Ian Nepomniachtchi) eight time World Blitz Chess Champion and the reigning Chess World Cup Champion.

5× World Champion Chess Legend
